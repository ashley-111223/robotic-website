What is Robotics?

Robotics is an interdisciplinary field of engineering and computer science that involves the design, construction, operation, and use of robots. Robots are programmable machines capable of carrying out complex actions automatically or semi-automatically.

History of Robotics

The concept of robotics dates back to early mechanical inventions. Modern robotics began in the 20th century with industrial automation. Today, robotics integrates Artificial Intelligence, sensors, and machine learning to create autonomous systems. History

Types of Robots

  • Industrial Robots: Used in manufacturing and assembly lines.
  • Service Robots: Assist humans in homes and public spaces.
  • Medical Robots: Support surgeries and rehabilitation.
  • Military Robots: Used for surveillance and hazardous missions.
  • Autonomous Robots: Operate independently using AI.

Core Components of a Robot

  • Sensors (to detect environment)
  • Actuators (to create movement)
  • Controller (microprocessor or computer)
  • Power supply
  • Software algorithms
